# SEO Audit — Neighbor Web Co.
**Domain:** neighborwebco.com  
**Date:** June 23, 2026  
**Audit type:** Full site audit

---

## Executive Summary

Neighbor Web Co. has a well-structured, clearly branded site with strong messaging, good mobile-first intent, and a solid internal linking structure. Canonical tags, OG tags, and most meta descriptions are in place — that's a real foundation. However, the site has three critical gaps holding it back from organic visibility: **no blog or content section** (meaning zero ability to rank for the high-volume informational queries your prospects search), **no industry-specific landing pages** (restaurant website design, plumber website design, etc.), and **no location pages** targeting San Diego or specific neighborhoods. The pricing page is also missing its meta description. The top priorities are: (1) launch a blog and publish 6–8 foundational posts, (2) build 4–5 industry landing pages, and (3) add city/location targeting throughout the site.

## Content Gap Recommendations

### 1. Launch a blog at /blog
**Why it matters:** There's no informational content on the site. Competitors and even larger platforms (Squarespace, Wix) rank for queries like "how much does a small business website cost" and "do I need a website." These are exactly the people you want — local business owners researching their options before buying.  
**Recommended format:** Blog, 800–1,200 words per post  
**Priority:** High  
**Effort:** Moderate (half-day per post)

**First 6 posts to publish (in order):**
1. "How Much Does a Small Business Website Cost? (Honest Breakdown)" — targets high-intent buyers at the research stage
2. "Custom Website vs. Website Builder: What's Better for a Local Business?" — directly addresses the Wix/Squarespace objection
3. "5 Signs Your Local Business Website Is Hurting You (and What to Do About It)" — awareness stage, very shareable
4. "What Makes a Great Restaurant Website? (With Examples)" — industry-specific, links to your portfolio
5. "How to Get More Calls and Bookings From Your Website" — practical, positions you as an expert
6. "Google Business Profile: The Free Tool Every Local Business Needs" — ties into your Local SEO Setup service

---

### 2. Industry-specific landing pages
**Why it matters:** Searches like "restaurant website design," "plumber website design," and "barbershop website design" are low-difficulty, high-conversion keywords. Your portfolio already shows work in all these categories — you just need pages to rank for them.  
**Recommended format:** Dedicated service landing pages (~600–800 words)  
**Priority:** High  
**Effort:** Moderate per page (half-day each)

**Pages to build:**
- /services/restaurant-website-design
- /services/home-service-business-website-design (covers plumbers, landscapers, cleaners)
- /services/bakery-website-design
- /services/barbershop-website-design
- /services/food-truck-website-design

Each page should: include the keyword in the title, H1, and meta description; link to relevant portfolio examples; and have a clear CTA to the free review.

---

### 3. Location page (San Diego)
**Why it matters:** The phone number has a 858 area code (San Diego). Searches like "web design San Diego small business" or "San Diego local business website" have moderate volume and are very targetable. No location page exists at all currently.  
**Recommended format:** Landing page at /san-diego-web-design (~600–800 words)  
**Priority:** High  
**Effort:** Quick win (2–3 hours)

---

### 4. Detailed case studies
**Why it matters:** The Work page lists 9 portfolio projects but they're all cards with no detail. Competitors who write 500-word case studies with results ("quote requests doubled in 2 months") rank for brand + result queries and build way more trust.  
**Recommended format:** Individual pages at /work/[project-name]  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Effort:** Moderate per case study

---

### 5. Comparison page
**Why it matters:** "Custom website vs Wix" and "why not just use Squarespace" are extremely common objections. A comparison page pre-answers them and captures commercial-intent searches.  
**Recommended format:** Blog post or landing page  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Effort:** Moderate (half-day)
